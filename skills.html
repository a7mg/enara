Communication Skills

Communication Skills aim to promote essential abilities for effective communication in children facing developmental delays. This includes understanding verbal and non-verbal information and enabling clear communication of thoughts and emotions. It assists in speech formation through clear pronunciation and fluency, while social communication involves engaging in effective interactions, such as listening and empathy. These skills are crucial for the personal, professional, and social development of special-needs children.

Cognitive Skills

Our interactive activities are designed to engage and challenge different areas of the child’s brain, including memory, attention, and problem-solving skills. By integrating visual and auditory stimulation, repetition, pattern recognition, and other techniques, Enara not only enhances children's cognitive skills but also boosts confidence and improves their overall quality of life.

Play Skills

Play skills are essential for children’s development and learning, involving purposeful engagement with toys, imaginative play, and symbolic representation. These skills promote active involvement in play with others and the use of visual aids to understand routines. These skills are vital for cognitive, social, and emotional development, nurturing creativity, problem-solving, and social interaction.

Motor Skills

This part helps improve a child’s physical abilities such as gross and fine motor skills, coordination, balance, and strength. Through enjoyable activities targeting these areas, children can enhance their overall physical development, leading to improvements in academics and social skills. These activities include throwing, catching, balancing, and manipulating objects for both fine and gross motor skill development.
